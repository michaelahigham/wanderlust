KUALA LUMPUR

Kuala Lumpur is the capital city of Malaysia. The city has it all: modern, gleaming skyscrapers, colonial architecture, collections of natural attractions and delicious food in the form of hawker stalls, restaurants and night markets.

There is something for all travellers that head to Malaysia. A good start may be visiting the 'Golden Triangle' - Bukit Bintang, KLCC and China Town. You can also travel to Little India.

Explore by yourself or book onto a tour - either way, Kuala Lumpur should make it on to your bucket list.

KUALA LUMPUR WEATHER

TOP 5 THINGS TO SEE IN KUALA LUMPUR

VISIT PETRONAS TOWERS

The PETRONAS Towers are the tallest twin towers in the world. They are one of the iconic images people associate with Malaysia. The brave can flock to the tower to walk over the sky bridge that joins the two towers - leaving you to admire the breathtaking views that reach across the city. If the thought of standing over the city feels you with some trepidation, you can also enjoy PETRONAS Towers in the green park at the base.

MARVEL AT SRI MAHAMARIAMMAN TEMPLE

Sri Mahamariamman Temple is not only beautiful, but it is also the oldests Hindu temple in all of Kuala Lumpur. Admire it fine detailing, learn about the diverse cultures and religions which build Kuala Lumpur as people know it. The temples colorful vibrant art depicts a variety of Hindu deities of legends. Ultimately, however, whilst you explore make sure to pay your respects as Sri Mahamariamman Temple as a working place of worship.

SLOW DOWN AT KUALA LUMPUR BUTTERFLY PARK

Escape the hustle-and-bustle of city life with a visit to the Kuala Lumpur Butterfly Park! Marvel at the hundreds of different colorful butterflies.. The park itself is modeled on a jungle forest experience and features walkways, footpaths, lakes, ponds, and gazebos. With beautiful scenery accompanied by educational explanations, visitors can relax. If you are a fan of nature, you can find tickets which combine the Bird Park, the Butterfly Park and the Orchid Garden.

CLIMB TO BATU CAVES

Venture just outside of Kuala Lumpur to visit Batu Caves. The caves and temples are Hindu shrines and are still considered an important place of pilgrimage for the many Tamil residents of Malaysia.

The multi-coloured stair-case is an eye catching and perfect “insta! spot. Visitors can experience wild monkeys that inhabit the area as well as famous cave-dwelling bats.

SHOP AT CENTRAL MARKET

Souvenirs are a staple for any holiday. Therefore, it is not surprising that the Central Market in Kuala Lumpur, also known as Pasar Seni, is a firm location on the tourist map.

A Malaysian cultural landmark, Central Market has been classified as a Heritage Site by the National Heritage Department. It can be compared to London’s Convent Garden.
