PORTO

Porto (Portuguese Oporto) lies along the Douro River. World-renowned for its port wine, Porto is the second largest city in Portugal. The picturesque, historic centre of Porto - Ribeira - gained UNESCO World Heritage status in 1996.

Like Lisbon, it is easy to arrive in Porto. Many low cost carriers operate routes from UK airports to Porto.

TOP 5 THINGS TO SEE IN PORTO

RELAX IN RIBERIA SQUARE

Porto's riverside quarter, known as the Ribeira, is one of the city's oldest neighbourhoods and certainly one of its highlights. It is a popular tourist attraction and it’s easy to see why! The area was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1996 and it does not disappoint.

The narrow, cobbled streets of Ribeira are dotted with small bars and eateries.

ENJOY THE FAMOUS DOURO RIVER

The Douro River cuts through Porto. Dom Luis I Bridge is the most famous way to cross the River. Built between 1881-85, the bridge evokes memories of the Effiel Tower, having both been designed by the French Civil Engineer Gustave Eiffel.

At its construction, its 172 metres (564 ft) span was the longest of its type in the world. Enjoy some people watching on this scenic river side.

FALL IN LOVE WITH LIVRARIA LELLO

The Livraria Lello bookstore in Porto is one of the world's oldest bookstores, frequently ranked as one of the most beautiful bookstores in the world, and a top place to visit in Portugal. It was built in 1906 by engineer Xavier Esteves.

All parts of this bookstore are magical. The ceiling is designed to mimic wood, with a stained-glass skylight inscribed with the store's motto "Decus in Labore," or "Honor in Labor." Many of the store pillars depict Pourtugese literary figures. The outside is no less beautiful.

Your entrance ticket also gives you £5 off a book purchase.

SIP PORT IN PORTO

Port Wine originated from the need to preserve the wine as it completed the long journey from Douro Valley to Porto and then to England. Various Port tours are available in Porto. Taste authentic port, learn how port is made, how it used to be transported. Your tour may even take you to Ferreira Cellars.

Established in an old convent, Ferreira Cellars is one of the most extraordinary wineries that you can visit in Gaia.

Interested in Port? Consider taking a day-trip to the iconic Douro Valley.

CLIMB CLERIGOS TOWER AND CHURCH

The Clérgios church was built between 1735 and 1748.

Clérigos Tower (Torre dos Clérigos in Portuguese) is the tallest campanile in Portugal. It stands 249 ft (76 meters) tall and climbing its 200 steps will give you a privileged view over the city and the river.
